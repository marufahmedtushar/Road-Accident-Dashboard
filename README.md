# Road-Accident-Dashboard
## Objective

Analyze accident trends and identify major causes.

## Dataset

66,000+ records

## Tools

Power BI
Excel

## KPIs

-Total Accidents

-Casualties

-Fatalities

-Road Type

-Weather

-Severity

**Casuality**

<img width="706" height="400" alt="accident 2" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2f9106fa-b235-4b47-a7a9-45e3dedec4a9" />

**Insights**

• 60.57% of all casualties occur in urban areas.

• Casualties are highest during daylight and on dry road surfaces.

• 13.35% of all accidents are classified as serious accidents.

• Most accidents occur during daylight rather than in darkness.

**Accident Severity**

<img width="707" height="395" alt="accident 3" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ef99c70c-72ed-4fef-bc26-e98fbe26c173" />

**Insights**

• Single carriageway roads account for the highest number of casualties.

• They also record the highest number of both slight and serious accidents.

• Although fewer accidents happen on wet roads, nighttime driving on wet roads presents a much higher safety risk.

**Location Analysis**

<img width="708" height="397" alt="accident 4" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6386bc89-42ad-47ee-9de6-2c030c7f97aa" />

**Insights**

• Birmingham is the most accident-prone district.

• Clackmannanshire records the fewest accidents.

• Urban areas experience significantly more accidents than rural areas.

• However, rural areas become considerably more dangerous at night, with a noticeable increase in accidents.

**Road & Weather Analysis**

<img width="709" height="396" alt="accident 5" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/88877533-e8d2-4257-8820-19f852b773a0" />

**Insights**

• Dry and wet road surfaces contribute to the majority of accidents, with dry roads accounting for the largest share.

• Surprisingly, the highest number of accidents occurs during fine weather.

**Overall Conclusion**

The analysis indicates that road accidents are most common during daylight, on dry roads, in urban areas, and under fine weather conditions, largely because these conditions coincide with higher traffic volumes. However, the highest-risk situations are nighttime driving in rural areas and on wet roads, where visibility and road conditions significantly increase accident severity.
